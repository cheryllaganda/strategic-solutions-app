Commercial Models

We offer flexible commercial models to suit different client needs and risk appetites. Explore the options to find the best fit for your innovation goals.

Tiered Access & Subscription Model (with "Freemium" Option)

Offer a limited "taste" of the Strategic Solutions Hub. This could include: access to thought leadership content (white papers, webinars, industry reports), participation in general Strategic Solutions Hub workshops/webinars, a one-time, high-level assessment of a specific process area (but not a full project), and access to a limited online knowledge base/community. The purpose is to generate leads, demonstrate expertise, and build relationships, serving as a low-risk entry point.

A monthly or annual subscription fee provides access to a defined number of "innovation credits" or hours per year, and participation in a set number of smaller-scale "incubation projects". The purpose is to provide ongoing value and build a recurring revenue stream, priced attractively below perceived value.

A higher subscription fee unlocks unlimited "innovation credits" or a significantly larger allocation, priority access to the Strategic Solutions Hub team, larger, more complex, and strategically important projects, and dedicated resources (e.g., a specific data scientist or engineer assigned part-time). The purpose is to cater to clients with significant innovation needs and a higher willingness to invest.

For projects exceeding allocated credits/hours, charge additional fees based on scope and complexity, with transparency about these costs. This allows for flexibility and ensures compensation for larger engagements.

Gain-Sharing Model (Performance-Based)

You agree with the client on specific, measurable KPIs that the Strategic Solutions Hub projects will impact (e.g., cost reduction, efficiency gains, revenue increase). A percentage of the achieved improvement (the "gain") is shared with your organization. For example, if the Strategic Solutions Hub helps the client reduce operational costs by $1 million, and the gain-sharing agreement is 20%, you receive $200,000.

Pros: Strongly aligns incentives; you're directly rewarded for delivering tangible results. Clients see a clear path to ROI, making them more willing to invest. Can be very profitable if projects are successful.
Cons: Requires careful negotiation of KPIs and measurement methodology upfront. Requires a high degree of trust and transparency. Your revenue is dependent on the client's success in implementing the Strategic Solutions Hub's recommendations. Measurement attribution can be complex.

Project Process Flow:

1. Engagement & KPI Definition: Identify opportunity and agree on measurable KPIs that the Strategic Solutions Hub projects will impact, and the gain-sharing percentage.

2. Contracting: Formalize KPIs, measurement methodology, and terms in a legal agreement.

3. Project Execution: The Strategic Solutions Hub implements the solution to impact the defined KPIs.

4. Performance Measurement: Post-implementation, monitor KPIs and calculate the value of the gains.

5. Verification & Reward: Present gains to the client for verification, then invoice for the shared percentage.

Co-Investment Model (Shared Risk, Shared Reward)

You and the client jointly invest in the Strategic Solutions Hub's infrastructure and project costs. This could be a 50/50 split, or any other agreed-upon ratio. Both parties share in the benefits (e.g., IP ownership, cost savings, revenue generated).

Pros: Demonstrates your commitment and "skin in the game". Reduces the client's upfront financial burden. Fosters a deeper partnership.
Cons: Requires a higher level of trust and collaboration. Can be more complex to structure legally and operationally. You share the risk if projects are unsuccessful.

"Innovation Fund" Model (Client-Funded, with Governance)

The client establishes a dedicated "innovation fund" managed by the Strategic Solutions Hub. The Strategic Solutions Hub proposes projects to be funded from this pool, and the client approves them based on strategic alignment and potential ROI.

Pros: Gives the client control over how their funds are used. Clearly separates Strategic Solutions Hub funding from other operational expenses. Facilitates long-term planning and budgeting for innovation.
Cons: Requires the client to commit a significant amount of capital upfront. You need a robust project selection and governance process to ensure the fund is used effectively.

Outcome-Based Pricing (Fixed Fee for Defined Outcomes)

Instead of hourly rates or project-based fees, charge a fixed price for achieving a pre-defined, measurable outcome. Example: "We will reduce your customer service call handling time by 15% for a fixed fee of $X".

Pros: Provides price certainty for the client. Focuses on delivering value, not just effort.
Cons: Requires a very precise understanding of the problem and the solution. You bear the risk if the project takes longer or is more complex than anticipated.

Offerings for Prospective Clients

Engage with our Strategic Solutions Hub through offerings designed to demonstrate value and build trust with minimal initial commitment.

Enhanced "Champion-Challenger"

A low-risk trial where our CoE team (the "Challenger") replicates a portion of a prospect's operations to compete against their current setup (the "Champion"). If we meet or exceed pre-defined success criteria in a set timeframe, the prospect commits to scaling with us. This is a powerful, data-driven way to prove our value.

CoE "Diagnostic" Assessments

A free or low-cost, focused assessment of a specific operational area. We use our CoE capabilities to analyze workflows, audit technology, and analyze data, delivering a report with findings and potential ROI. It's a valuable deliverable even without a larger contract.

CoE "Showcases" & Demonstrations

Instead of generic demos, we create highly targeted demonstrations within the CoE that address a prospect's specific pain points; including "Day in the Life" simulations of their workload.

CoE "Innovation Sprints"

Short, focused 1-2 week engagements to solve a specific problem. Working collaboratively with the prospect's team, we deliver a tangible output like a prototype or proof-of-concept.
